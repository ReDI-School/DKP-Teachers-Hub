## 📢 **Recruitment Phase**

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
### Meet & Greet | Introduction to ReDI Kids

This friendly 1:1 interview is part of our volunteer admission process. It helps us understand your experience, motivations, and availability, and gives you space to ask questions. We use it to see if volunteering at the ReDI Kids is a good fit for you.&#x20;

Teaching experience is a plus, but not required. What matters most is your availability, commitment, responsibility to the teaching team, and motivation to keep learning and support students.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Fill out the Official Registration

**Your journey as a volunteer teacher has begun!**\
After the Meet\&Greet, you will receive an email with the official [Volunteer Registration Form](https://redischool.typeform.com/dcp-application). If you haven’t filled out the yet, we recommend doing it now. It’s a key step in your application and we need it in order to activate your volunteer role. It also ensures you don’t miss any important information and allows us to prepare everything for your registration. _It will only take about 4 minutes._

{% embed url="https://redischool.typeform.com/dcp-application" %}
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Self-Onboarding Process

After filling out the official **Volunteer Registration Form**, you’ll be officially in our system. Shortly afterwards, you’ll receive an automated email with a link to a page that contains important information. Please read everything carefully and follow the steps listed in the **Self-Onboarding** section.

Once you’ve completed the steps in the self-onboarding process, you’ll be all set to start the semester.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Sign your Volunteer Agreement

Once you complete the self-onboarding process, you’ll receive your Volunteer Agreement. Please take some time to read it carefully, and if you have any questions or comments, don’t hesitate to reach out to the Volunteer Coordinator at [**tatiana@redi-school.org**](mailto:tatiana@redi-schooll.org). If everything looks good, go ahead and sign it. 😃
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Fill out your availability!&#x20;

Once we have the teams ready, and you're in the Slack channel, we'll share with you our **Organizational Spreadsheet**. This document contains:&#x20;

* **Teaching team spreadsheet:** this is where you can mark your availability and where we’ll organize the teaching teams for each Sunday.&#x20;
* **Drive with last semester’s content (for reference):** includes slides and Kahoot links.
* **Drive for this semester:** please use this folder to save any new content you create. Feel free to upload any additional materials you prepare here.
* **Credentials:** for all the important accounts.

The Organizational Spreadsheet is also saved in the **Slack channel bookmarks**, so you can quickly access it whenever you need.
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

## 🗂️ **Preparation Phase**

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
### Kick-off Session

**Spring Semester:** April 19th

The goal of this session is to meet finally on-site, for you to meet the rest of the team members, review the course content and align on roles, responsibilities, and collaboration to ensure a strong learning experience for students.

* **Preparation:** Review the course content, structure, roles, and available tools in advance.
* **Timing:** Usually covered in one meeting, though a second session may be scheduled if needed.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Stay Connected on Slack

From this point on, **all team communication** happens in **Slack**, so please make sure it’s easily accessible—ideally on your phone. You can download it here:

[iPhone](https://slack.com/intl/en-gb/downloads/ios) | [Android](https://slack.com/intl/en-gb/downloads/android)

_Please be generous with the reactions, so we know our messages are reaching you. 💙🥳_
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

## 🧑‍🏫 **Teaching Phase**

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
### Semester Kick-Off

**Spring: the first official day of the semester is April 26th.**

The semester lasts 8 sessions plus Demo Day.&#x20;
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Bi-Weekly Planning Sessions

To coordinate roles, review content, and discuss any adjustments, each teaching team (Ninjas, Heroes, and Superheroes) will hold a short **30-minute planning session**. During this session, you will:

* Decide the roles for the upcoming two Sundays
* Make any necessary content adjustments
* Share questions, concerns, or ideas as a teaching team

Before the semester starts, you will receive a **Slack poll** to vote on the best day and time for your team’s planning session.

**Attendance is mandatory** for all volunteers who will be teaching in the upcoming **two Sundays**. You will receive a reminder in Slack.

If, in a rare case, you cannot join the session but are still scheduled to teach, please:

* Inform your team which role you will take
* Share any questions or concerns about the content beforehand

This ensures everyone is prepared and confident for the Sunday classes! 👏
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Teachers' Training

**When?** During the 2nd and 3rd week of the semester, depending on our trainers availability.&#x20;

**Once per semester**, we offer a **training session** led by our pool of experienced trainers, who generously volunteer their time to support the ReDI volunteer community. These sessions are carefully designed to help you thrive in your role as a teacher and as part of the teaching team.

We hope you find them valuable! If there are specific topics you’d like to explore or if you’d like to connect with a trainer for future semesters, don’t hesitate to reach out to our Volunteer Coordinator at [tatiana@redi-school.org](mailto:tatiana@redi-school.org).
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Teachers' First Impressions

**When?**  During the 3rd and 4th week of the semester

At this stage, we will reach out to gather your initial impressions of your experience as a teacher volunteer. We are interested in your feedback on the teaching team, students, ReDI team, materials, and overall organization, as well as any early ideas for improvement.&#x20;
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Mid-Term Social Event&#x20;

Halfway through the semester, we’ll take some time to **celebrate** all the incredible work you’ve been doing, relax, and enjoy some fun moments together as a team. More details about the format and activities will be shared closer to the date.&#x20;
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

## 🚀 Final Project Phase & Demo Day

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
### Final Project Preparation

During the last session of the semester, you’ll help the kids prepare the projects they’ll present on **Demo Day** and guide them in practicing their presentations. This session is the perfect opportunity to boost their confidence and showcase all they’ve learned!

Please let us know promptly if there’s anything the ReDI team needs to prepare to ensure all students’ projects are ready for the big day.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Demo Day Celebration! **🎉**&#x20;

**Spring Semester:** June 21st

**Where?** We usually choose a special location to make Demo Day memorable.

Demo Day is the semester’s closing celebration, where students present their projects to their relatives. It’s a celebration day where students, parents, volunteers, and the ReDI team come together to celebrate the semester closing. Students receive their certificates of completion, and we play some fun games to end the semester on a high note.

**Important:** All volunteers are expected to be present on Demo Day to support the students and make the celebration extra special!
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

## 🔄 Continuation

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
### Teachers' Final Feedback

**When?** After Demo Day

This is the final survey of the semester, where we would like to hear your thoughts after having the full experience as a teacher volunteer with us. Your feedback, ideas for improvement, and any challenges you encountered are essential to continuously improve the experience for future semesters.
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Continuation Form

We will send a survey to ask whether you would like to **continue as a teacher volunteer for another semester** and if you are interested in supporting ReDI in other ways, such as mentoring, trainings, supporting the teaching team, or promoting our programs. We are happy to have you remain an active part of our community. An early response of this form will support us in the recruitment process for the next semesters since we give priority to our current teachers.
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

# 🤖 Teaching Roles

What roles are available during classes?

🧊 ICEBREAKER: It’s the volunteer who prepares the warm ups, energizer games in the class&#x20;

📖PRESENTER: It is the volunteer who present the slides/ content

💻EXERCISER: It is the volunteer who prepares the exercises according to the slides

🏆KAHOOT MASTER: It’s the volunteer who prepares the Quiz Game Kahoot

How many hours should I expect to invest in each role?

The Icebreaker: Prepares the warm ups, energizer games in the class.&#x20;

Approx. workload:  \~30–45 min prep

The Presenter: Volunteer who presents the slides/ content.

Approx. workload: \~1–1.5 hours prep

The Exerciser: Prepares the exercises according to the slides.

Approx. workload: \~1–1.5 hours prep

Kahoot Master: Prepares the Quiz Game Kahoot.

Approx. workload: \~30–45 min prep

#### How do we decide roles each week?

As a volunteer, you can choose a different role for each Sunday you join. For example, you might be the Presenter one week and the Icebreaker the next. The distribution of roles depends on the teaching team scheduled for that Sunday, and your preferences.&#x20;

To organize this, each teaching team holds a bi-weekly planning call. In this call, you’ll:

* Decide who takes which role for the upcoming class
* Share important updates about the students (e.g. special needs or learning challenges)
* Coordinate adjustments, such as repeating a topic or adapting the pace

This way, everyone is prepared, and the class runs smoothly.

The day and time of this planning call will be agreed on during the first Planning Session, based on what works best for the majority of the team.
